Topic 1: Ethics of AI

The use of AI in law enforcement is associated with philosophical, contextual, and situational complexities and considerations. AI applications in law enforcement include crime pattern analysis, telephone record analysis, profiling, threat assessment, and strategic targeting of criminals. The philosophical schools of deontology and utilitarianism provide conflicting views on the integration of AI into law enforcement practices. Deontology promotes rule-based ethics, while utilitarianism advocates for actions that benefit the majority in a population. Therefore, deontologists are likely to oppose the use of AI in law enforcement due to the associated risk of cognitive privacy violations. However, utilitarianism could advocate for the application of AI to reduce crime and enhance the security for the majority of the population. Moreover, different situations and contexts determine the risk of ethics violations in AI law enforcement applications. However, the use of AI in crime pattern analysis and threat assessment has a limited risk of rights infringement.
